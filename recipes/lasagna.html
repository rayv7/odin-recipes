Delicious Lasagna Recipe

About the Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide pasta sheets with rich tomato sauce, creamy cheese, and hearty fillings like meat or vegetables, then baking it to golden perfection. It's versatile, easy to customize, and perfect for feeding a crowd since it can be prepared in advance. Cooking lasagna builds useful kitchen skills, fills your home with amazing aromas, and delivers a comforting, impressive meal that tastes even better the next day.

Ingredients

  • lasagna noodles
  • ground beef
  • onion and garlic
  • herbs and spices
  • ricotta cheese
  • egg
  • mozzarella cheese
  • permesan cheese
  • zucchini

Steps:

  1. Prepare the meat sauce

    Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic, sauté until fragrant and soft. Stir in ground beef (or beef/pork mix), cooking until browned. Drain excess fat if needed, then add tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes and a spoon of tomato paste. Season with basil, oregano, salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar if the sauce tastes too acidic. Let it simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes to deepen the flavor.

  2. Prepare the ricotta mixture

    In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese with one beaten egg. Add a handful of chopped fresh parsley, a pinch of salt, and a little Parmesan cheese. Mix well.

  3. Cook the noodles

    If using dried lasagna noodles, boil them until just tender (al dente), then drain and lay them flat on parchment or lightly oiled foil to prevent sticking. If using oven-ready noodles, you can skip boiling.

  4. Assemble the layers

    In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom. Add a layer of noodles, then spread some ricotta mixture on top, sprinkle mozzarella, and add more sauce. Repeat the layers: noodles → ricotta → mozzarella → sauce, until ingredients are used up. Finish with a final layer of noodles topped with meat sauce, mozzarella, and a generous sprinkle of Parmesan.

  5. Bake the lasagna

    Cover the dish loosely with foil (to prevent burning) and bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 30-40 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 10-15 minutes until the top is bubbly and golden brown.

  6. Rest and serve

    Let the lasagna sit for at least 10-15 minutes after baking — this helps it set and makes slicing easier. Slice into squares and serve warm.
